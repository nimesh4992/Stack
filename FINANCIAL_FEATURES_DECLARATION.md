### Question 3: Does your app process payments?

**Answer:** No

**Explanation:** The app only tracks and records financial transactions. It does not process any actual payments or transfers.

---

### Question 4: Does your app connect to financial institutions?

**Answer:** No

**Explanation:** The app reads bank transaction SMS messages locally on the device to detect transactions. It does not connect to any bank APIs or financial institution systems.

---

### Question 5: Is your app regulated or licensed?

**Answer:** Not required

**Explanation:** HabitFinance is a personal finance tracking tool and does not provide regulated financial services such as lending, investment advice, or payment processing. It is similar to budgeting apps like Mint or YNAB.

**Note:** If you're operating in specific regions (India, Thailand, Indonesia, etc.) with strict financial app regulations, you may need to provide additional documentation.

---

### Question 6: How does your app handle financial data?

**Answer:** All data is stored locally on user's device only.

**Details:**
- Financial transaction data is stored in encrypted local storage
- No data is transmitted to external servers
- User controls all data through in-app settings
- Data can be deleted by uninstalling app or using Clear Data option

## Additional Declaration for SMS Usage

### Question: Why does your app need SMS access?

**Answer:**
HabitFinance uses SMS reading to automatically detect and log bank transaction alerts. This is a convenience feature that:

1. **Purpose:** Auto-detect bank transactions (debits, credits, UPI payments)
2. **Processing:** All SMS parsing happens on-device only
3. **Data Handling:** No SMS content is transmitted or stored externally
4. **User Control:** Feature is optional and can be disabled anytime
5. **Disclosure:** Prominent disclosure is shown before permission request
